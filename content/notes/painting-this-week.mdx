Six paintings finished between Saturday and Tuesday. The fastest stretch I have had since I started painting seriously.

What surprised me: tempera. I had been working acrylic and charcoal only, and on a whim I cracked a tube of cheap student tempera over the weekend. It dries flat and matte in a way acrylic does not, and it sits underneath charcoal beautifully — the powder catches on the matte surface and you can keep pushing it without the paint going slick.

What also surprised me: spray. I bought one can of cheap matte black to outline a figure and instead used it as ground — a thin pulled drift of black down the right side of a canvas, and the figure read against it like a shadow puppet. I will be using more.

What I am taking forward: the four by six format. The figures sit better at this size. They have room to be both close and far.
